Research Project Experience

Natural Gas Consumption Forecasting Using DL and MSTL

The project uses Multi-seasonal trend decomposition to decompose the time series of gas consumption volume into multiple seasonal components, a trend component, and a residual component. The trend and residual component are proceeded by global and local convolution, further followed by self-attention with mlp. The result is concluded by depersonalization of the seasonal components and the forecast results of trend and residual components.

Fault Diagnosis Using DL and Signal Processing

The project proposed a two-step bearing fault diagnosis method. The first stage uses GAN to check out whether the bearing is at fault status. If a fault is detected, the second step is to proceed which further uses the signal analysis method to check out the characteristic frequency to locate where the fault is.
